What is nitrous oxide?

Nitrous oxide (N2O), also called "laughing gas", is a colorless, odorless gas that is used as a sedative (making patients relax) prior to medical procedures. In dental procedures, it is mixed with oxygen and inhaled through a mask that fits over your nose and mouth. [1][2] It can also be used in baking - cartilages of whipped cream chargers containing nitrous oxide (typically 8g) are inserted into a whipped cream dispenser to create whipped cream.

Although nitrous oxide has many uses, many of which are safe, this gas can be inhaled to achieve an euphoric "high." In order to "inhale" nitrous oxide, one may discharge a cartilage of nitrous oxide into a balloon, and then inhale the balloon. These are the same "balloons" that one may inhale at a party. Nitrous oxide is perfectly legal - thousands can be bought online using a single transaction, making it one of the easiest substances to access. This is why nitrous oxide is dangerous - it is easy to access.

When nitrous oxide is inhaled, a pleasant and euphoric "high" can be felt within a few seconds and disappears within minutes. Because nitrous oxide acts as a sedative, it "calms" the user down. After this "high" occurs, however, side effects may follow. We'll talk more about the side effects at the How N2O works + its effects section.

DSM-5 Diagnosis

Nitrous oxide doesn't have a specific disorder (i.e. there is no such thing in the DSM-5 that defines "N2O use disorder". However, N2O is classified in the "other substance use disorders" category. According to this category, fulfillment of 2-3 of the criteria below indicate mild substance use disorder, 4-5 criteria indicate moderate substance use disorder, and more than 6 criteria indiate severe substance use disorder.

How N2O works + its effects

Usage of nitrous oxide can lead to a lot of complications. When nitrous oxide is inhaled, a phenomenon known as diffusion hypoxia may occur, where the nitrous oxide settles into the bodyâ€™s cavities faster than nitrogen can come out of the body, which may lead to death. Another serious effect nitrous oxide has on the body is that it is able to irreversibly oxidize and deactivate the vitamin B12 parts of certain enzymes, such as methionine synthase [5] , which results in a vitamin B12 deficiency in the body [6].

Methionine synthase is a crucial enzyme to produce methionine, which makes up the myelin sheaths of neurons. When the shape of methionine synthase is altered because its vitamin B12 parts are oxidized, it can't carry out its function well. As a result, production of methionine is affected, thus leading to lack of myelin sheath production. Myelin sheaths "wrap" around nerves, and are responsible for speeding up nerve impulses to the brain. What does this mean?

Neurons are responsible for forming a "bridge" for nerve "messages" to travel to the brain. For example, when someone throws a ball at you at full speed, your first instinct would be to dodge it. The reason why this is because neurons are "telling" your brain that a ball is headed your way, and the brain works to move any necessary muscles for you to dodge the ball.
This vitamin B12 deficiency may cause nerve damage, which leads to tingling and numbness, and at times, muscle weakness and dizziness [7] .
